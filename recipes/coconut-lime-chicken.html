Coconut-Lime Chicken


Description

This recipe pairs chicken cutlets and coconut milk to deliver an out-of-this-world, tasty treat!

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 2 large chicken breasts
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 tsp EACH ground ginger, garlic powder, ground coriander, turmeric powder, pepper

To Cook

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 green onions. chopped white and green parts separated
  • 2 teaspoons freshly grated ginger or 3/4 tsp ground
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced or 1 tsp ground

Coconut sauce

  • 1 13.5oz can quality coconut milk
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup Asian sweet chili sauce like Mae Ploy
  • 3-4 tablespoons honey (start with 3)
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon reduced sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Asian chili sauce more or less to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil

Steps

  1. Bring 3 cups of water to a boil in a medium pot, then add 2 cups of rice and turn the heat down to
    simmer. Put the lid on and leave covered for 20 minutes.
  2. On a cutting board that has space, prep aromatics. Chop onion and sweet peppers, then put a pan on
    the stove at about half heat. Oil generously.
  3. When the pan is hot (oil will start to steam), add in your chopped veggies along with half a cup of
    the pre-chopped carrots. Season with salt, garlic, and chili powder.
  4. Stir veggies occasionally for ~ 10 minutes then lower the heat. Remove them from the pan, and place
    somewhere to the side.
  5. Turn the heat back up to medium-high, and prep your steak. Add garlic, salt, and pepper. Oil again,
    and place in the pan. For a perfect medium-rare, cook each side for two minutes. Do not fiddle with it!
  6. Bring heat to low, then add in your butter. Once completely melted, tilt the pan so it pools on one side.
    With a spoon, scoop the butter on to your steak repeatedly for about 2 minutes. This is known as
    basting.
  7. Keep steak pan on low heat, and turn off the heat to your rice. Let everything sit for an additional 5
    minutes, then remove your steak from the pan. Do not cut into it or touch it!
    (It is still cooking from the inside.)
  8. Your rice should be perfectly cooked now. Remove lid and scoop a third of it into the steak pan we
    just used. Add in the veggies we cooked earlier to reheat them. The excess oil and fat from the steak is
    what will be turning our white rice, into fried rice. If there isn't enough oil, feel free to add more to
    the pan.
  9. Once rice is golden brown, turn off heat and scoop everything into a dinner bowl of your choice. You
    can now cut your steak into medium-sized bites, and add them into your bowl.
  10. Drizzle your sauce of choice over top, (mine is teriyaki) , then squeeze your lime over everything.
    That's it! Enjoy!
